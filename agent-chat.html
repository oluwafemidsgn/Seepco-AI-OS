What is the emergency shutdown procedure for Bonga FPSO?
10:14 AM

Based on SOP-OPS-007 Rev.4, Bonga FPSO Operations Manual §12

The Emergency Shutdown Procedure for Bonga FPSO (SOP-OPS-007, Rev. 4 — updated March 2024) defines a three-tier shutdown sequence:

  • Level 1 — Process Shutdown (PSD): Initiated automatically by process control systems or manually by the operator. Isolates specific process equipment while maintaining overall facility operations. No prior authorization required.
  • Level 2 — Emergency Shutdown (ESD): Requires authorization from the Offshore Installation Manager (OIM). Shuts down all production operations and isolates hydrocarbon inventories. NAPIMS must be notified within 2 hours.
  • Level 3 — Emergency Support Station (ESS): Full facility shutdown including utilities. Dual-authorization required from OIM + Area Authority. Triggers automatic evacuation protocol and SIMOPS suspension.

The March 2024 revision introduced updated communication checklists and a dual-authorization requirement for Level 3 shutdowns.

Who has authority to authorize a Level 3 shutdown?
10:17 AM

Based on SOP-OPS-007 Rev.4 §3.3, Authority Matrix 2024

Per the updated SOP-OPS-007 (Rev. 4), a Level 3 Emergency Support Station (ESS) shutdown requires dual-authorization from:

  • Offshore Installation Manager (OIM) — primary authorization
  • Area Authority (typically the VP Operations or delegated representative onshore) — secondary authorization

In situations where the Area Authority is unreachable, the OIM may invoke the Emergency Authority Protocol and initiate the shutdown unilaterally, with immediate notification required once communication is restored.

This dual-authorization requirement was introduced in the March 2024 revision following a lessons-learned review.
