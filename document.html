The Emergency Shutdown Procedure for Bonga FPSO (SOP-OPS-007, Rev. 4) defines a three-tier shutdown sequence — Level 1 Process Shutdown, Level 2 Emergency Shutdown (OIM authorization, NAPIMS notification within 2 hrs), and Level 3 ESS (dual-authorization, full evacuation).

SOP-OPS-007 Rev.4 — Emergency Shutdown Procedure

Emergency Shutdown Procedure — Bonga FPSO

SOP-OPS-007 Rev.4  |  Effective: March 2024  |  Supersedes: Rev.3 (June 2022)

1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure defines the emergency shutdown sequence for Bonga FPSO, including authorization requirements, communication protocols, and post-shutdown checklists. It applies to all personnel with operational authority aboard the facility.

2. Scope

Applicable to all Bonga FPSO operations and support personnel. This revision supersedes SOP-OPS-007 Rev.3 and incorporates lessons learned from the 2023 unplanned Level 2 shutdown event.

3. Shutdown Levels

Cited

3.1 Level 1 — Process Shutdown (PSD)

Initiated by: Process control system (automatic) or Operator (manual)
Authorization: No prior authorization required
Scope: Isolates specific process equipment or trains while maintaining overall facility operations.

Level 1 shutdowns are routine and managed at the operator level. An entry must be made in the Shift Log within 30 minutes of initiation. No external reporting obligation unless the shutdown results in a production loss exceeding 500 bbl/day.

Cited

3.2 Level 2 — Emergency Shutdown (ESD)

Initiated by: Offshore Installation Manager (OIM) or Control Room Supervisor
Authorization: OIM authorization required
Scope: Full shutdown of all production operations and isolation of all hydrocarbon inventories.

  • NAPIMS must be notified within 2 hours of initiation (Form NAPIMS-IR-01)
  • Onshore Operations Centre notified within 30 minutes
  • DPR notified if shutdown exceeds 24 hours

Emergency Response Team (ERT) to be placed on standby. All non-essential SIMOPS to be suspended immediately.

Cited

3.3 Level 3 — Emergency Support Station (ESS)

Initiated by: OIM with Area Authority co-authorization (introduced Rev.4)
Authorization: Dual-authorization required — OIM + Area Authority
Scope: Full facility shutdown including all utilities; automatic evacuation protocol triggered.

  • Automatic muster alarm triggered on ESS initiation
  • All personnel to designated Muster Stations within 10 minutes
  • Medevac/emergency vessel on standby upon L3 declaration
  • NAPIMS, DPR, and NOSDRA notification required within 1 hour

Emergency Authority Protocol: If Area Authority is unreachable, OIM may invoke unilateral initiation and must notify Area Authority at first available opportunity.

4. Communication Protocol

All external notifications shall be made using the Standard Incident Notification Form (SINF-01). Communications log must be maintained throughout the event and submitted as part of the Post-Incident Report.

5. Post-Shutdown Requirements

Following any Level 2 or Level 3 shutdown, a Post-Shutdown Review must be completed within 24 hours of facility restart. Root Cause Analysis (RCA) is mandatory if the downtime exceeds 4 hours. RCA findings to be submitted to HSSE Director within 72 hours.

6. Revision History

Rev.4 (March 2024): Added dual-authorization requirement for Level 3 shutdowns (§3.3). Updated NAPIMS notification timeline. Added Emergency Authority Protocol.

Rev.3 (June 2022): Aligned with updated DPR reporting requirements.
